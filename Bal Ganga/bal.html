Early Life

Bal Gangadhar Tilak was born on July 23, 1856, in Ratnagiri, Maharashtra, India.

He was a scholar and a nationalist.

Bal Gangadhar Tilak

Indian Nationalist and Freedom Fighter

Born: July 23, 1856

Died: August 1, 1920

Tilak was a key figure in the Indian independence movement.

Freedom Movement

Tilak was a strong advocate for swaraj (self-rule) and swadeshi (indigenous industries).

He founded the Indian Home Rule League and was imprisoned several times for his activism.

Legacy

Tilak's legacy as a freedom fighter and nationalist continues to inspire Indians today.

He is remembered as a hero and a champion of Indian rights.
